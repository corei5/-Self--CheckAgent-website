Abstract

Both education and science depend on reasoning over complex problems and testing hypotheses to interpreting results and verifying correctness. With the rapid rise of large language models (LLMs), AI systems are increasingly assisting in these domains, offering support for mathematical and scientific problem-solving, reasoning, and educational tutoring. Recent studies show that over 70\% of students and researchers now use LLMs for academic purposes, particularly in mathematics. %zhu2024embracing However, LLMs are prone to generating hallucinations — outputs that sound plausible but are factually incorrect which threatens the reliability of AI tools applied for educational and scientific contexts. Building on this need, we are developing the TIB AI Assistant, an agentic AI tool designed to support learners and researchers. Its goal is not just to deliver answers but to ensure factual reliability -- a critical challenge given the prevalence of LLM hallucinations. In this paper, we present the Agentic SelfCheck Framework, a novel multi-agent framework for detecting hallucinations in LLM-generated content. By addressing the specific needs of educational and scientific tasks, the Agentic SelfCheck Framework lays the groundwork for more trustworthy and effective AI assistants.
